PCCB (Protein Centroid Conformer Bank)

In the PCCB we are aiming to store major conformers discovered through out molecular simulation. These are generated either through molecular dynamics or monte carlo methods. The major conformers from systems simulated typically are derived from a simulation ran from an initial protein conformation derived from the protein data bank. These conformers are useful for various reasons. They represent the major conformations (large percentages of the simulation time) observed throughout the simulation. Significantly large conformations may be stabilised by ligands of interest. Uses include:

  • These conformers for instance could be utilised for multi-receptor docking.
  • Starting points for molecular dynamics or monte carlo.
  • Creation of community free energy landscapes for systems backed by reproducible science.
